# Czech Masér (NSK 69-037-M) Exam Curriculum

**Qualification:** Rekondiční a sportovní masér/masérka (Reconditioning and Sports Massage Therapist)  
**Standard:** NSK 69-037-M, Level 4 (ISCED 4)  
**Governing Law:** Zákon č. 179/2006 Sb. (Further Education Results Verification); registered via Zákon č. 455/1991 Sb. (Trade Licensing Act)  
**Primary Source:** NSK 69-037-M Hodnotící Standard (https://www.narodnikvalifikace.cz/kvalifikace-320-Rekondicni_a_sportovni_maser_maserka/hodnotici-standard)

---

## Exam Structure

- **Total Duration:** 3–4 hours (excluding 15-minute prep)
- **Written Component:** 75 minutes
- **Assessment Commission:** 2 authorized examiners
- **Pass Requirement:** All six domains must be "splnil" (fulfilled); failure in any domain = overall failure

---

## Six Exam Domains

All domains have **equal weight** in the exam. No explicit percentage breakdown is provided by NSK; however, based on assessment criterion count and exam time allocation, estimated coverage is ~17% per domain.

---

### 1. CLIENT COMMUNICATION & BOOKING MANAGEMENT
**Estimated Coverage:** ~13% (8 assessment criteria)  
**Assessment Methods:** Oral examination, practical demonstration  
**Sources:** NSK 69-037-M § Domain 1

#### Sub-Topics:
- **Client Scheduling & Appointment Management**
  - Booking procedures and cancellation policies
  - Time management and scheduling efficiency
  - Appointment confirmation and reminder systems
  
- **Health History & Intake Procedures**
  - Medical questionnaire completion and interpretation
  - Contraindication screening (when NOT to massage)
  - Consent and client autonomy
  
- **Communication Protocols**
  - Active listening and empathetic response
  - Explanation of massage benefits and techniques
  - Setting realistic expectations for results
  
- **Special Populations Communication**
  - Elderly clients with hearing or mobility issues
  - Clients with language barriers
  - Clients with anxiety or trauma history
  
- **Documentation & Record-Keeping**
  - Client database management (GDPR-compliant per Nařízení EU č. 679/2016)
  - Progress notes and session summaries
  - Confidentiality and privacy compliance

### 2. PHYSICAL ASSESSMENT (ANATOMY & PHYSIOLOGY)
**Estimated Coverage:** ~17% (5 assessment criteria + 200-question written test)  
**Assessment Methods:** Written exam (3 criteria, 70% pass threshold), practical/oral (2 criteria)  
**Written Test:** Minimum 200 multiple-choice questions, **70% correct answers required**  
**Sources:** NSK 69-037-M § Domain 2; Vyhláška č. 306/2012 Sb. § Health & Safety

#### Sub-Topics:

##### 2.1 — Skeletal System (косте產)
- Bone classification (long, short, flat, irregular)
- Major bones and bony landmarks (skull, spine, ribs, pelvis, limbs)
- Joints and joint types (ball-and-socket, hinge, pivot, condyloid)
- Ligaments and connective tissue
- Common skeletal pathologies (osteoporosis, arthritis, fractures)
- Palpation landmarks for massage assessment

##### 2.2 — Muscular System (svalová soustava)
- Muscle classification and function (skeletal, cardiac, smooth)
- Major skeletal muscles and anatomical groups
- Muscle actions (flexion, extension, abduction, adduction, rotation)
- Origin and insertion concepts
- Muscle contraction types (isometric, isotonic, eccentric)
- Common muscle injuries (strains, tears, myofascial pain)
- Muscle palpation and assessment during massage

##### 2.3 — Respiratory System (dýchací soustava)
- Anatomy: nasal cavity, trachea, lungs, diaphragm
- Breathing mechanics and ventilation
- Gas exchange principles
- Respiratory pathologies relevant to massage (asthma, COPD)
- Client positioning for respiratory comfort during massage

##### 2.4 — Circulatory System (oběhová soustava)
- Heart anatomy and cardiac cycle
- Arteries, veins, and capillaries
- Major blood vessels and pulse points
- Blood pressure and cardiovascular regulation
- Lymphatic system basics
- Contraindications: deep vein thrombosis, varicose veins, hypertension
- Massage pressure and direction (respecting venous/arterial flow)

##### 2.5 — Skin & Contraindications (kůže a kontraindikace)
- Skin anatomy (epidermis, dermis, hypodermis)
- Skin conditions and dermatology basics
  - Infections (fungal, bacterial, viral)
  - Inflammatory conditions (eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis)
  - Wounds, burns, scar tissue
- When NOT to massage (contraindications)
  - Acute inflammation or infection
  - Undiagnosed pain or swelling
  - Recent surgery or injury
  - Skin diseases (contagious or open wounds)
- Client skin assessment and communication

### 3. HEALTH & HYGIENE COMPLIANCE
**Estimated Coverage:** ~17% (5 assessment criteria)  
**Assessment Methods:** Oral examination (4 criteria), practical demonstration (1 criterion)  
**Sources:** NSK 69-037-M § Domain 3; Vyhláška č. 306/2012 Sb.; Zákon č. 258/2000 Sb. § 19–22

#### Sub-Topics:

##### 3.1 — Facility Sanitation & Standards
- Minimum facility requirements (24 m² massage room)
- Cleaning schedules and cleaning agent selection
- Ventilation and air quality standards
- Lighting and temperature control
- Water supply and drainage standards
- Waste management and biohazard disposal

##### 3.2 — Equipment Disinfection & Sterilization
- Classification of equipment (critical, semi-critical, non-critical)
- Disinfection methods (chemical, heat, UV)
- Single-use vs. reusable equipment policies
- Massage table hygiene and protective coverings
- Towel and linen handling and laundry standards
- Oil and lotion container safety

##### 3.3 — Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) & Hygiene
- Glove selection and proper donning/doffing
- Hand hygiene protocols (washing, sanitizing)
- Protective clothing and dress codes
- Personal cleanliness standards
- Illness policy (when employees should not work)

##### 3.4 — Epidemiologically Significant Activity Compliance
- Understanding "epidemiologically significant activity" classification per Zákon č. 258/2000 Sb.
- Operational regulation (operační řád) approval by regional hygiene station (KHS)
- Documentation requirements for activities
- Incident reporting for potential exposures

##### 3.5 — Occupational Health & Personnel Screening
- Annual medical examinations and fitness certification
- Health screening documentation
- Vaccination status and compliance (per current Czech requirements)
- Infectious disease exclusion criteria
- Health monitoring and incident reporting

### 4. MASSAGE PERFORMANCE (RECONDITIONING & SPORTS MASSAGE)
**Estimated Coverage:** ~17% (8 assessment criteria)  
**Assessment Methods:** Oral examination (technique description), practical demonstration (full-body massage)  
**Key Requirement:** "Popsat a provést celkovou masáž, na všech částech těla ve správném pořadí"  
**Sources:** NSK 69-037-M § Domain 4

#### Sub-Topics:

##### 4.1 — Upper Extremity Massage
- Shoulder girdle (trapezius, rhomboids, deltoid, rotator cuff)
- Arm and forearm muscles (biceps, triceps, flexors, extensors)
- Wrist and hand massage
- Hand positions and pressure techniques
- Adapting to client comfort and pain levels

##### 4.2 — Lower Extremity Massage
- Hip and gluteal muscles
- Thigh muscles (quadriceps, hamstrings, adductors)
- Calf and tibial region
- Ankle and foot massage
- Techniques for balance and proprioceptive feedback

##### 4.3 — Trunk & Spinal Region Massage
- Paraspinal muscles and erector spinae
- Latissimus dorsi and thoracic muscles
- Abdominal region considerations
- Rib cage and intercostal muscles
- Pressure and technique variations for deep vs. superficial work

##### 4.4 — Neck & Head Region
- Cervical musculature (sternocleidomastoid, scalenes, upper trapezius)
- Neck safety and contraindications
- Temporal and jaw muscles
- Scalp massage techniques
- Carotid artery awareness and pressure avoidance

##### 4.5 — Massage Techniques & Modalities
- Swedish massage strokes (effleurage, petrissage, friction, tapotement, vibration)
- Trigger point work and myofascial release
- Deep tissue vs. relaxation massage
- Timing and rhythm (cadence and pressure variation)
- Integration of breathing and client feedback

##### 4.6 — Full-Body Massage Sequence & Efficiency
- Logical progression across all body regions
- Transition techniques and flow
- Time management (completing full-body in ~45–60 minutes)
- Client positioning and draping
- Energy conservation for therapist sustainability

##### 4.7 — Adaptations for Special Populations
- Elderly clients with mobility limitations
- Clients with acute vs. chronic pain
- Pregnant clients (positioning and pressure)
- Clients with anxiety or trauma sensitivity
- Adaptive techniques for limited range of motion

##### 4.8 — Client Feedback & Safety
- Verbal and non-verbal feedback recognition
- Pressure adjustment during treatment
- Communication during massage ("Is this pressure okay?")
- Recognizing contraindication signs and stopping treatment
- Post-massage recovery and hydration guidance

### 5. FIRST AID & EMERGENCY RESPONSE
**Estimated Coverage:** ~17% (5 assessment criteria + 70-question written test)  
**Assessment Methods:** Written exam (3 criteria, 70% pass threshold), practical demonstration (2 criteria)  
**Written Test:** Minimum 70 multiple-choice questions, **70% correct answers required**  
**Practical Test:** Resuscitation demonstration on approved training mannequin (CPR)  
**Sources:** NSK 69-037-M § Domain 5; Zdravotnický zákon č. 372/2011 Sb. (where applicable)

#### Sub-Topics:

##### 5.1 — Basic Resuscitation & CPR
- Adult CPR: chest compressions (rate, depth, hand position)
- Adult CPR: rescue breathing technique
- Recovery position and airway management
- Defibrillation (AED) use
- Choking recognition and abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver)

##### 5.2 — Loss of Consciousness & Shock
- Recognizing unconsciousness (responsiveness assessment)
- Causes of shock (hypovolemic, anaphylactic, septic)
- Shock symptoms (pale, rapid pulse, shallow breathing)
- Shock treatment (lay flat, elevate legs, monitor, call emergency)
- Seizures: recognition and safety measures

##### 5.3 — Injury Assessment & Management
- Fracture recognition and splinting
- Sprain vs. strain vs. fracture differentiation
- Severe bleeding control and tourniquet use
- Wound assessment and dressing
- Burn classification and cooling techniques
- Suspected spinal injury protocols (immobilization, no movement)

##### 5.4 — Emergency Communication & Response
- When to call emergency services (112 in Czech Republic)
- Information to provide to emergency dispatcher
- Keeping client calm and reassured
- Staying with client until professional help arrives
- Documentation of emergency incident

##### 5.5 — Common Medical Emergencies
- Chest pain / possible heart attack
- Severe allergic reaction / anaphylaxis
- Diabetic emergency (hypoglycemia vs. hyperglycemia)
- Stroke recognition (FAST: Face, Arms, Speech, Time)
- Heat exhaustion vs. heat stroke
- Hypothermia

### 6. BUSINESS OPERATIONS & COMPLIANCE
**Estimated Coverage:** ~17% (8 assessment criteria)  
**Assessment Methods:** Practical demonstration, oral examination  
**Sources:** NSK 69-037-M § Domain 6; Zákon č. 455/1991 Sb.; Nařízení EU č. 679/2016 (GDPR); Czech Tax Code

#### Sub-Topics:

##### 6.1 — Business Registration & Licensing
- Bound trade (vázaná živnost) registration requirements
- Required documentation for trade office (živnostenský úřad)
- NSK 69-037-M qualification proof
- Trade license maintenance and renewal
- Compliance with trade regulations

##### 6.2 — Financial Management & Pricing
- Service pricing strategy (cost-plus, market-based, value-based)
- Revenue tracking and invoicing
- Payment methods and client billing
- Price transparency and communication
- Discounts and package pricing

##### 6.3 — Tax Compliance & Documentation
- Business income registration (příjmy z podnikatelské činnosti)
- Expense documentation (equipment, supplies, utilities, education)
- VAT status and exemption considerations
- Tax return filing (daňové přiznání)
- Record-keeping and accounting software
- Work with tax advisor or accountant

##### 6.4 — GDPR & Client Privacy Compliance
- Personal data definition and client database protection
- Consent forms for data collection and processing
- Data storage security (encrypted systems, secure backup)
- Client rights: access, correction, deletion ("right to be forgotten")
- Data breach notification procedures
- Privacy policy document (in Czech, client-accessible)
- Third-party data processors (e.g., appointment scheduling software)

##### 6.5 — Client Database Management
- Appointment scheduling system setup
- Client intake forms (health history, contraindication screening)
- Session notes and progress tracking
- Secure data storage (password-protected, encryption)
- Data retention policies (how long to keep historical records)
- Backup and disaster recovery procedures

##### 6.6 — Insurance & Liability
- Professional liability insurance (pojištění odpovědnosti za škodu)
- Coverage for injury during treatment or on-site incident
- Insurance policy review and adequacy assessment
- Incident documentation for insurance claims
- Waivers and liability release forms (Czech legal requirements)

##### 6.7 — Employment & HR (if hiring employees)
- Employee hiring and legal contracts
- Workplace safety standards
- Working hours and compensation
- Social insurance contributions (sociální pojištění)
- Health and safety training
- Record-keeping for employees

##### 6.8 — Continuous Professional Development
- Ongoing education and recertification requirements (if any)
- Specialized training (sports massage, therapeutic techniques)
- Professional association membership (optional)
- Conference attendance and skill development
- Staying current with health and safety regulations

## Exam Pass Requirements & Logic

✓ **All six domains are mandatory.**  
✓ **All six domains carry equal weight** in the overall assessment.  
✓ **Failure in any single domain = overall failure** ("nevyhověl").  
✓ **Explicit percentage exam breakdown** is NOT provided by NSK; all domains must be mastered.  
✓ **Written tests (Anatomy 200 Qs, First Aid 70 Qs) require 70% correct** to pass that criterion.  
✓ **Oral and practical criteria** are assessed by the exam commission (subjective, criterion-referenced).

## Notes on Exam Preparation

1. **Written Tests:** Anatomy (200 Qs) and First Aid (70 Qs) are 70% pass-threshold. Budget significant study time for these.
2. **Practical Massage:** The full-body massage sequence (Domain 4) requires hands-on practice. Attend accredited training courses.
3. **Oral Examination:** Commission will ask follow-up questions on anatomy, technique, safety, and legal compliance. Clear explanations are essential.
4. **Domain Integration:** Topics often overlap (e.g., contraindications span Anatomy, Hygiene, and Massage Performance).
5. **Czech Language:** All exams are conducted in Czech. Use Czech terminology for anatomy, legal concepts, and clinical terms.
